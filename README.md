## The Three Types of Spanish Proxies (And Which One You Want)

Before you compare prices, you need to know which type you're shopping for. Picking the wrong category is the most expensive mistake people make.

### Datacenter Spanish Proxies

These IPs come from servers in Spanish data centers. They're fast — often faster than your home internet — and they're cheap. The catch? Sophisticated anti-bot systems canometimes flag datacenter IPs because the IP ranges are publicly known to belong to hosting providers.

**Best for:** SEO tracking, general scraping of less-protected sites, accessing geo-locked content where the platform doesn't actively block datacenter ranges, high-volume tasks where sped matters more than anything else.

### Residential Spanish Proxies

These route your traffic through real residential internet connections in Spain — actual home broadband from regular Telefónica, Vodafone, Orange, or Yoigo subscribers. To any website, this traffic is indistinguishable from a real Spanish user browsing from their living room.

**Best for:** Scraping protected sites (Amazon ES, sneaker drops, ticket platforms), social media management, ad verification, anything where you absolutely cannot get flagged.

### Static Residential /ISP Proxies

A hybrid. The IPs come from real ISPs (so they look residential), but they're hosted in data centers (so they're fast and stable). They don't rotate. The same IP stays with you as long as you want it.

**Best for:** Account management, long-running sessions, anything that needs to look residential but also requires a consistent IP for login persistence.

Quick recap so you don't have to scroll back: datacenter is cheap and fast, residential is stealthy and pay-per-GB, ISP is the expensive middle ground. Pick based on what you're actually doing — not what sounds fanciest.
